Setup

  • If playing on sand, use the paddle to draw two squares approximately 6'x6' and separated by 6'.
  • If playing on grass or other similar surface, use the included rope and spikes to create a 6'x6' box.
  • One player stands in each box with a paddle.

Gameplay

  • Play begins by one of the players serving by hitting the ball underhand towards the opponent's square. The other player must return the sever underhand towards the first player's square.
  • The players must return the ball to each other a total of four times without faulting before attacks (overhand hits) can be attempted: Serve, return, return, return, ATTACK! The player who served will be the player who can attack first.
  • During this initial phase, if either player does not return the ball, a fault is given. 
  • The fault is given to the player who hit the ball if the ball lands outside the box of the receiving player. The fault is given to the receiving player if the ball lands inside the receiving player's box. If either player receives two faults, a point is assigned to the other player who then also will serves.
  • Between each serve the score is called out by the server by saying their own score as well as the fault count. For example, "1-0, my fault." Or "4-5, my fault, your fault."
  • After the initial four hit portion of the game, the players may begin to attack by using over or underhand hits.
  • If a player his the ball and it lands inside the square of their opponent then they are awarded a point and then serve.
  • If a player hits a ball and it lands outside of the square of their opponent then the other player is awarded the point and the serve.
  • Play continues to 11 points.
